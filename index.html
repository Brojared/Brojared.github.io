About Me

Hi, I'm Jared.

The motivation behind this portfolio is to capture everything I am proud of and present it in a way that feels authentic to me. This covers everything from late nights spent on class projects to weekends pursuing my other hobbies. While I know a single website cannot capture everything I have worked toward, I hope that those who make their way through it can see that I bring full commitment to every position I have been in.

I have always appreciated computer science ever since elementary school, and over the years I have explored a wide range of subcategories within the field to find where my true passion lies. I have worked on projects spanning everything from low-level systems design to applying AI libraries in Python. These roads have led me toward data science and AI as my current focus.

My internship at Aspirion, working with healthcare data and preparing it for use in AI workflows, taught me that the level of scrutiny and organization that data requires is genuinely interesting work. Beyond that, I have taken courses that take raw data and present it in meaningful ways through interactive dashboards and visualizations. Together, these experiences make me confident operating across any branch of a data-driven organization, from collection and engineering all the way to presentation and insight.

This portfolio is organized to give you a full picture of who I am. You will find my resume, a few of the projects I am most proud of, the hobbies that keep me grounded outside of work, and the social profiles where you can follow along with what I am building. Whether you are a recruiter, a collaborator, or just curious, I hope something here is worth your time.

Academic & Professional Work

Motor Vehicle Insurance Claims — ML Classification

Built a binary classification model on a 105,000+ record insurance dataset to predict whether a customer would file a claim. Applied feature engineering, KNN imputation for missing values, and SMOTE to address class imbalance. A Decision Tree Classifier achieved 74.6% accuracy and 80.8% precision, with customer seniority emerging as the strongest predictor of claim likelihood.
